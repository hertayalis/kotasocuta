What Happened to 105.9 The Oasis? 105.9 The Oasis and History

July 2024 · 3 minute read

Let’s tune in and unravel the story of 105.9 The Oasis – the radio station that’s been making waves in Middletown, Ohio. You might be wondering, “What Happened to 105.9 The Oasis?” Well, hang tight as we explore the station’s history and the recent twists and turns in its journey.

What’s the Scoop on 105.9 The Oasis?

Once known as WNKN, 105.9 The Oasis was your go-to spot for classic hits, taking you on a nostalgic ride through the golden eras of the ’60s, ’70s, and ’80s. But hold on – change is the name of the game. In July 2022, it shook things up by switching from classic country to classic hits. A rebranding occurred, giving birth to “105.9 The Oasis” and a fresh musical experience.

Fast forward to August 2023, and here comes the plot twist. News broke that WNKN was about to embark on a new journey, leaving its classic hits format behind. Why? Because it was announced that the station would be sold to Relevant Radio for a cool $4.5 million. Ownership change alert! Brace yourselves for upcoming alterations as the station is expected to transition to a different format while simulcasting with WNKR. It’s all part of the dynamic nature of the radio industry, where change is the only constant.

Taking a Trip Down Memory Lane – 105.9 The Oasis History

Now, let’s hop into the time machine and explore the history of 105.9 The Oasis. The station’s roots trace back to 1959 when it kicked off as WPFB-FM, the first FM station in Ohio to broadcast in FM stereo. Throughout the years, it danced through various music styles – soul, R&B, disco, and country.

In 2011, there was a significant shift towards a non-commercial, listener-supported adult album alternative (AAA) sound, simulcasting with WNKU. Changes kept coming, and by 2017, it returned to the classic country format. But wait, there’s more! In 2022, another evolution happened, transforming it into the classic hits format we know today as “105.9 The Oasis.”

As we step into 2023, the plot thickens once again. The station is on the brink of being sold to Relevant Radio, marking another chapter in its ever-evolving history. This journey reflects how radio stations like 105.9 The Oasis adapt to listeners’ preferences and the ever-changing media landscape.
