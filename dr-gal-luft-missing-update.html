Dr. Gal Luft Missing Update

The U.S. government has issued a warrant for missing Israeli academic Gal Luft, who disappeared on Tuesday, April 25.

The Jerusalem Post reports that Luft was detained at Cyprus’ Larnaca International Airport after being accused of smuggling weapons into China and Libya.

He was due to be extradited to the U.S., but after a court in Larnaca granted him bail, he vanished.

On March 28, Gal Luft’s formal warrant was issued when he failed to attend a Paphos Police station. Later, he denied being an arms dealer in a tweet, saying the accusations were false and part of a plot by the U.S. government.

He continued by saying he is a target because of his criticism of U.S. foreign policy.

The loss of Luft has upset his loved ones, close friends, and coworkers. Michael Rubin highlighted worries about the Gal Luft missing case and the possible repercussions of his disappearance in an opinion piece published in JNS.

According to Rubin, the allegations against Luft may have something to do with his disappearance, but there are other possibilities, such as political pressure or private motives.

Luft is a well-known authority on energy security and has participated in numerous public policy discussions.

Dr. Gal Luft Missing: Where Is The Israeli Professor? 

Although there has been no official word on Luft’s whereabouts, his family and friends are still holding out hope that they will be able to locate him.

The family of Luft has reportedly recruited a private detective to assist in the search, according to a story by The Jerusalem Post.

To find Luft, the investigator claimed he coordinates closely with officials in Cyprus and other nations.

The investigator acknowledged that identifying Luft would be difficult, given the limited resources and the chance that he might have departed Cyprus.

The mystery surrounding his disappearance grows more complicated with various rumors regarding Luft’s potential whereabouts. The Times of Israel reported that Luft was last observed in the region of Cyprus that Turkey controls.

Reports have also indicated that he might have escaped Cyprus and is currently in another nation.

There have also been rumors that Luft might have been abducted or killed, although there is no proof to back up these accusations.
